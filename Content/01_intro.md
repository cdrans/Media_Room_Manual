# Welcome to the Media Room

Welcome! This manual is your guide to the **Teaching Lab Media Room** — a dedicated facility designed to help academic staff produce high-quality digital learning resources for students. Whether you are creating course content for the first time or looking to expand your existing materials, this room and the guidance within this manual are here to support you.

---

## The Facility

The Teaching Lab Media Room houses three purpose-built stations, each designed to produce a different style of learning resource:

- 🎙️ **Podcast Recording Station** — A acoustically treated setup for recording high-quality audio content, ideal for spoken discussion, interviews, and reflective or thought-provoking material.
- 🎬 **Greenscreen Video Station** — A camera and lighting setup in front of a greenscreen, allowing you to appear alongside your existing PowerPoint slides in a clean, professional video format.
- 🔆 **Lightboard Station** — A transparent illuminated glass panel and specialised markers that let you write and draw while facing the camera, replicating the experience of a traditional whiteboard lecture.

Each station produces a distinct type of media, and each type of media serves different pedagogical purposes. The sections that follow in this manual provide detailed step-by-step guidance for using each station. Before diving in, the overview below will help you decide which station is the right fit for the learning content you have in mind.

---

## Choosing the Right Station

What station is right for you? The reality is that not every media format is well suited for every pedagogical need. By clicking on the tabs below, you can learn more about the strengths of each station along with example outputs to help you make an informed choice of what is right for your needs.


::::{tab-set}

:::{tab-item} 🎙️ Podcast

Podcast recordings produce audio-only (or audio-led) content that students can consume flexibly — during a commute, a walk, or any moment away from a screen. Because there is no visual component, podcast content works best when the subject matter itself is the focus: discussions, debates, reflective prompts, interviews, and conceptual ideas that benefit from a student being fully immersed in listening and thinking.

**Best suited for:** Discussion-based topics, opinion and argumentation, reflective or philosophical content, and any material where visuals would add little value.

:::{tab-item} 🎬 Greenscreen Video

The greenscreen station lets you record yourself presenting directly alongside your PowerPoint slides, producing a polished video that students can watch, pause, rewind, and replay at their own pace. This format is particularly effective for structured, scripted content where clarity and conciseness are priorities. Because the video can be reviewed non-linearly, it suits material where students may need to revisit specific points.

**Best suited for:** Structured explanations, module introductions, summarising key concepts, and any content that already has an accompanying slide deck.

:::{tab-item} 🔆 Lightboard

The lightboard replicates the natural pace and feel of a traditional whiteboard or blackboard session. Writing and drawing in real time — while remaining visible and facing the camera — creates a sense of presence that is well suited to material requiring deliberate, step-by-step explanation. This format tends to produce longer videos than scripted greenscreen recordings, so it is most valuable when that slower, unfolding pace is a genuine pedagogical asset rather than a constraint.

**Best suited for:** Mathematical problem-solving, derivations, diagrams, processes with multiple sequential steps, and any content where working through the material slowly is itself part of the learning.

## How to Use This Manual

Hopefully by now you are inspired to make use of the Teaching Lab Media Room and start creating dynamic educational content. Once you have identified the station that best fits your content, navigate to the corresponding chapter for a full walkthrough using the navigation menu on the left or through the links below:

- **Podcast Recording Station**
- **Greenscreen Video Station**
- **Lightboard Station**

Each chapter covers room setup, equipment operation, recording guidance, and export/file management. If you are unsure which format to choose, the Teachiong Academy is available to offer advice before your session.

We hope this facility helps you create engaging, effective learning resources for your students and that you enjoy the process of making them.
